# Li Lu's Value Investment Framework

Based on Li Lu's 2006 masterclass at Columbia Business School.

## The 5-Minute Analysis

Li Lu emphasizes speed and mental math: **"Each page shouldn't take you more than a few minutes."**

### Step 1: Quick Check (30 seconds)
1. Check P/B ratio (looking for < 1.5x book value)
2. Check P/E on pre-tax operating earnings (looking for 2-5x)
3. Market cap vs. cash (looking for < 2x, ideally < 1x!)

### Step 2: Operating Quality (1 minute)
1. Revenue scale
2. Operating margin (pre-tax, pre-interest)
3. Operating earnings = Revenue × Margin
4. **Calculate RODC** (target: > 50% = exceptional)

### Step 3: Capital Structure (2 minutes)
1. Separate liquid vs. deployed assets
2. Calculate deployed capital (exclude ALL cash)
3. Verify RODC calculation

### Step 4: Margin of Safety (1 minute)
1. Tangible book value
2. Real estate and fixed assets
3. Downside protection

## The Investment Checklist

> "You don't think of yourself as a paper-shuffler when you buy a stock, but as an owner of the business."

### Question 1: Is it cheap?
- **P/B < 1.5x** (ideally < 1.0x)
- **P/E < 10x** on pre-tax operating earnings (ideally 2-5x)
- **Market cap < cash** (rare but exists!)

### Question 2: Is the business good?
- **RODC > 50%:** Exceptional (Li Lu's target)
- **RODC 30-50%:** Strong competitive position
- **RODC 15-30%:** Good business
- **RODC < 15%:** Weak or capital-intensive

> "Your return on deployed capital is around 50%. That is not a bad business."

### Question 3: Is management trustworthy and good?
**Deep Due Diligence Required:**
- Read ALL shareholder lawsuits cover to cover
- Visit the family's community, church, synagogue
- Talk to neighbors, friends, business associates
- Spend weeks integrating yourself
- Check court documents
- Verify character through multiple sources

**Timberland example:**
> "I discovered one of the most admiring families I have ever met. People of the highest integrity and brilliant businessman."

### Question 4: What else am I missing?
- Why is it cheap?
- What are the risks?
- Asian crisis exposure? Industry headwinds?
- Visit stores, check product quality
- Test the business yourself

### Question 5: Why does this opportunity exist in the market?
- No analyst coverage?
- Crisis/panic selling?
- Foreign market (Korea, etc.)?
- Complex corporate structure?
- Family control concerns?

## Position Sizing Philosophy

> "Think about all the effort you put in. How good it is and your downside protection... So I put shitloads of money into it."

**When conviction is high:**
- 30-50%+ positions are not unusual
- Concentration is key for exceptional returns
- "If you wouldn't buy at that price, you would sell"

**Two Schools of Value Investing:**

1. **Graham/Tweedy Brown School:**
   - Diversified (20-30+ positions)
   - Statistical approach
   - ~15% annual returns
   - Lower risk through diversification

2. **Buffett/Munger School:**
   - Concentrated (5-10 positions)
   - Insight-driven
   - Multi-bagger potential
   - "Most of your money comes from very few insights"

Li Lu follows the second school:
> "In 50 years of tremendous insights into companies, there will probably only be ten or so insights."

## Historical Case Studies

### Case Study 1: Timberland (Fall 1998)

**Context:** Asian Financial Crisis, market panic

**Discovery:**
- Found in Value Line
- Stock: $28-30/share
- No analyst coverage
- Family controlled (40% equity, 98% voting)
- Multiple shareholder lawsuits

**Quick Analysis:**
```
Sales:              $850M
Operating Margin:   13%
Operating Earnings: $110M ($850M × 13%)

Book Value:         $300M
  - Liquid Assets:  $200M (including $100M cash)
  - Fixed Assets:   $100M (real estate)

Deployed Capital:   $200M ($100M WC + $100M PP&E)
RODC:               55% ($110M ÷ $200M)

Market Cap:         ~$275-300M
P/E:                ~2.7x (on pre-tax earnings)
P/B:                ~1.0x
```

**Due Diligence:**
- Read every shareholder lawsuit (discovered they were about missing guidance, not fraud)
- Visited family's community, church, synagogue
- Talked to neighbors and friends for weeks
- Son was on board with Li Lu's friend
- Visited stores - kids saw Timberland as fashionable

**Risk Assessment:**
- Asian exposure: Only 10% of sales, 27% international total
- Even discounting all Asian sales = only 5% earnings hit
- Strong retail momentum visible in stores

**Position:**
> "So I put shitloads of money into it"
(13F filings showed 30%+ positions weren't unusual for him)

**Outcome:**
- **7x return in two years**
- Growth matched earnings growth (not multiple expansion)
- P/E stayed under 15x
- Sold when analyst coverage increased

**Key Takeaway:** High RODC (55%) + cheap valuation (1x book, 2.7x P/E) + trustworthy management + crisis mispricing = exceptional opportunity

### Case Study 2: Hyundai Department Store H&S (Korea, ~2004-2005)

**Discovery:**
- Found reading Standard & Poor's Korea manual page by page
- Stock: $12/share
- 5.5M shares outstanding
- Market Cap: **$60 million**

**Quick Analysis:**
```
Pre-tax Earnings:   $36M
Net Income:         $25M
P/E:                2x (!)

Book Value:         $240M
  - Cash/Securities: $70M (ALL liquid!)
  - Fixed Assets:    $180M
```

**Deep Dive - Asset Breakdown:**

**Cash:**
- $70M in cash and marketable securities
- **Market cap ($60M) < Cash ($70M)!**
- Trading BELOW cash value (Hyundai H&S example!)

**Hotel (100% owned):**
- Book value: $30M
- After property inspection: $90-120M
- Real value: 3-4x book

**Department Store Stake (13%):**
- Book value: $13M
- Department store market cap: $600M
- 13% stake market value: **$80M**
- Hidden value: $67M above book

**Total Asset Value:**
- Cash: $70M
- Stocks (at market): $100M
- Hotel (at market): $150M
- **Total: ~$320M**

**Investment:**
- **Paying: $60M**
- **Getting: $320M+ in assets**
- **Annual earnings: $30M+**

**Risks Assessed:**
- Korea corporate governance (family-controlled)
- No foreign institutional ownership
- Required local market research

**Outcome:**
- Department store: $22 → $100 (5x)
- Hyundai H&S: $12 → $70 (6x after stock split)

**Key Takeaway:** Trading below cash + hidden asset values + $30M annual earnings for $60M cost = "no-brainer"

## Key Principles

### On Being a Value Investor

> "Only a small number of people are value investors and that is maybe because you are genetically mutated. You are very comfortable being in the minority - and this is not natural for humans."

### On Analysis Speed

> "Analyzing the numbers should take less than five minutes. If you are not a good analyst, you will not be a good investor. You must train these skills to be proficient."

**Required Skills:**
- Mental math (no calculators!)
- Pattern recognition
- "Smell" for opportunities
- Academic researcher mindset
- Investigative journalist approach

### On Mistakes

**His biggest mistakes weren't losses, but missed opportunities:**

> "Usually you make a mistake because you haven't done all the work, but that is ok... If you have tremendous insight and do not act on it, that is the big mistake."

**Example:** Missed a 50-100x opportunity when distracted by hedge fund short-selling:
> "It is not about the money I lost, but how much money I had forgone."

### On Selling

**Early philosophy:**
> "If I wouldn't buy at that price, I would sell."

**Evolved philosophy:**
> "I find myself being a real owner, and as a result not willing to let go of the business without earning a premium."

**For exceptional businesses:**
- If RODC is 50-100% and can reinvest at those rates
- Taxes from selling reduce capital for reinvestment
- Only sell if lacking confidence or if no enduring advantages

### On Exceptional Companies to Hold Forever

> "They need to have a competitive advantage that is getting stronger and stronger... the winner takes it all."

**Example: Bloomberg**
- High switching costs
- Network effects (everyone uses same platform)
- Monopolistic characteristics
- Gets stronger over time

### On Market Inefficiency

> "As long as humans are irrational, a value investor will find something profitable in the market. The market is designed for people, who are flawed in their thinking and are attracted because they like to trade and make fast money."

### The Lollapalooza Effect (from Charlie Munger)

> "Combine a lot of insights and factors... Sound, complete and accurate information combined with the conscious, subconscious and psychological factors."

Multiple factors combining:
1. Cheap valuation (statistical)
2. Good business (quality)
3. Trustworthy management (character)
4. Crisis mispricing (psychology)
5. Hidden assets (analysis)

When all align = exceptional opportunity

## Mental Models

### 1. Business Owner vs. Paper Holder
- Think in absolute dollars (market cap, earnings)
- Never in per-share terms during analysis
- Ask: "Would I buy the whole company at this price?"

### 2. Circle of Competence
- Build knowledge over 15+ years
- Study companies and industries deeply
- Learn from everything: biology, physics, history, poetry

### 3. Opportunity Cost
> "It is ok to have a year where you don't invest into a company, it is not ok to waste my time not learning and not creating a new insight."

### 4. Winner-Takes-All Dynamics
- High switching costs
- Network effects
- Competitive advantages that strengthen over time
- Monopolistic characteristics

### 5. Margin of Safety
- Demand huge protection (can't control the business)
- Tangible assets as downside
- Cash as cushion
- Real estate and fixed assets verification

## Action-Oriented Philosophy

> "Opportunities don't come very often, so you have to seize them. You have to get your research done quickly and accurately... Put your money in the bank until you have the opportunity to jump in and you have to devote intense work for a short, concentrated time period."

**The Process:**
1. Build knowledge continuously (years)
2. Scan for opportunities (daily)
3. 5-minute analysis when something interesting appears
4. 2-3 weeks of intense deep dive if it passes
5. Large position if conviction is high
6. Hold for years if business is exceptional

**Expected Returns:**
- Regular opportunities: 3-7x in 2-5 years (Timberland, Hyundai)
- Rare insights: 50-100x over decades (Bloomberg-type companies)
- Career: Maybe 5-10 transformational insights in 50 years

**Success Formula:**
- Deep work > shallow diversification
- Concentration > scattered bets
- Patience > activity
- Conviction > hedging
- Business owner mindset > trader mentality
